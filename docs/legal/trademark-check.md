# Trademark risk assessment — "Consilience"

**Status:** preliminary. A live web/USPTO search could not be completed in the session this was written (search rate limit). This records the knowledge-based assessment and the exact steps to finish clearance before any **commercial** launch. For the portfolio project, "Consilience" stands as the working name.

## The name

"Consilience" is an existing English word — coined by William Whewell (1840) and popularized by E.O. Wilson's 1998 book *Consilience: The Unity of Knowledge*, meaning the "jumping together" of knowledge across disciplines. For this product the term is **suggestive** (it evokes independent evidence converging) rather than descriptive, which is a registrable and reasonably strong category of mark.

## Risk: **moderate**

- Because it is a real word with intellectual cachet, "Consilience" is used by multiple existing entities — consultancies, a ventures firm, and at least one established software company in the telecom / lawful-intercept space. This creates **moderate crowding**.
- The relevant trademark classes for an AI research SaaS are **International Class 9** (downloadable software) and **Class 42** (software as a service). Risk turns on whether a live, registered mark in those classes covers services that overlap with "AI-powered research and analysis software."
- A same-class registration for overlapping services would create a genuine likelihood-of-confusion concern; uses in unrelated classes (e.g., consulting) generally would not block a software mark.

## What a USPTO search must verify

Using the USPTO trademark search system at [uspto.gov](https://www.uspto.gov) (the current replacement for TESS):

1. Search the exact term `CONSILIENCE`, plus phonetic and spelling variants (e.g., `CONSILIENT`, `KONSILIENCE`).
2. Filter to **live** marks (drop dead/abandoned) in **Class 009** and **Class 042**.
3. Read each live mark's goods/services description for overlap with AI research/analysis software or SaaS.
4. Note status: registered, pending, or under opposition.
5. Also run a common-law check (Google, product directories, GitHub, app stores, domain registrars) for unregistered marks in the same space.

## Recommendation

- **Portfolio use:** proceed as "Consilience."
- **Before commercialization:** complete the search above, and if a same-class overlapping mark exists, either obtain counsel's clearance or adopt a more distinctive **coined** variant to reduce risk (e.g., a made-up mark such as "Consilio" or "Converjent," which are inherently stronger and less crowded than a dictionary word). A coined mark is easier to register and defend.
- Secure the matching domain and social handles once the name is finalized.
